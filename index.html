The Digital Governance Design Studio is an initiative of the Duke Center on Law and Tech. We help communities build trustworthy relationships with the technology they depend on.
Mission

The DGDS is modeled on the growing "law school lab" movement: experimental spaces built on a hypothesis about how the practice of law is changing, and how future lawyers need to adapt. Here's ours.

Beneath the headline-grabbing anxiety about big tech's dominance, there is an urgent demand from organizations and communities who don't consider themselves "technology first"—health networks, legal aid, local government, civil society, and more—for help navigating complex data and technology issues.

Future lawyers must meet this need, no matter their practice area. Our mission is to empower law students and lawyers to do so, and to help "technology second" organizations attached to a digitizing world.

Work

DGDS incubates interdisciplinary projects that bridge the gap between academia and practice.

We collaborate with outside partners to help them build and evaluate trustworthy digital governance. We produce experiential classes and training materials to support the next generation of responsible data stewards, advisors, and governance experts. And we develop tools and infrastructure to support public interest technology projects.

Projects

Data governance class: health networks We’re designing a practicum class on data governance, to launch in Fall 2021. The first iteration of the course will focus on health data governance, and will match groups of students with partner health networks to solve digital governance challenges.

Evaluating governance We’re building tools and frameworks for evaluating and improving digital governance initiatives.

Causeway We’re building infrastructure to help preserve the public purpose of digital projects.
